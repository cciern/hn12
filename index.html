The Program

Find the most exceptional builders at your university. Give them a reason to show up.

HackNation runs one of the largest student hackathons in the world. HN12 is the human layer underneath it.

How you do that is up to you. A founder dinner in someone's apartment. A pitch night in a lecture hall. A co-founder speed-dating event. A weekend build sprint. Whatever works for your campus, your people, your style.

01

2 Campus Events

You get $1,000 to make them happen. Food, venue, whatever you need. If you land a local sponsor to cover costs, the savings roll into the cohort trip fund.

02

2 HackNation Hackathons

You lead the hub at your university. Bring your people in, host the teams. Fully funded centrally — you don't pay for anything.

03

Scouting

You know who's building what on your campus. When you find someone exceptional, you bring them into the community — into the hackathon, into the network, and if they're ready, into HackNation's accelerator.

The Fellowship

A six-month fellowship.
Here's what it covers.

  • $1,000 for your two campus events. Food, venue, drinks — whatever the event needs.
  • Full central funding for two HackNation hackathons at your university.
  • $500 floor on the end-of-cohort trip — Mexico or Colombia — with unlimited upside. Every dollar the twelve of you raise above event costs flows into the trip fund. 25% of any unrestricted sponsorship you personally land goes into your own travel fund.
  • Bi-weekly sessions with founders, operators, and investors. Linn Bieske (Head of Product, Waymo) is hosting one. Group format, with 1:1 time when the work warrants it.
  • The sponsor network — OpenAI, Databricks, Lovable, ElevenLabs, Mozilla. Warm intros when you need them.
  • Eleven peers at the other top universities, operating at your level.
  • Branded merch — cap, hoodie, t-shirt, laptop stickers.

Why Twelve

"Twelve is not arbitrary. It's roughly the largest group where every person still knows every relationship in the room. Past that, subgroups form, people hide, accountability dissolves. We picked twelve because it's the last number where no one disappears."

Based on Dunbar's social layers research (Oxford) and group cohesion studies.

HN12 exists because HackNation's accelerator is building the next cohort of AI-native founders. That doesn't happen through applications. It happens through people on the ground who know which sophomore at MIT just shipped something insane, which PhD at ETH is about to drop out.

"You need to make yourself a big target for luck, and the way to do that is to be curious. Try lots of things, meet lots of people, read lots of books, ask lots of questions."

— Paul Graham, "How to Do Great Work"
